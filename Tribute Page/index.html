Tribute

Nikola Tesla

Quote

“If you want to find the secrets of the universe, think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration.”

Biography

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a Serbian American inventor and engineer who shaped modern electrical engineering with his work on alternating current and countless other breakthroughs.

Early Life

Tesla was born in Smiljan, Croatia. His curiosity about electricity began with his mother, who built simple household devices. After studying in Europe, Tesla moved to America to pursue his dreams.

Inventions

Tesla worked briefly with Thomas Edison but soon pursued his own path. He introduced the Tesla Coil, AC power systems, and many more innovations that continue to power our world today.

Legacy

Though Tesla died in relative obscurity, his influence is celebrated worldwide. Streets, museums, and even electric car companies honor his name and his extraordinary contributions.

Notable Works

  • 1886

    Alternating Current

    The foundation of today’s electrical systems, making power transmission practical across distances.

  • 1891

    Tesla Coil

    A high-voltage transformer still used in radios, televisions, and experiments.

  • 1896

    X-ray Experiments

    His early shadowgraph work helped pave the way for modern medical imaging.

  • 1897

    Radio Concepts

    Tesla’s work preceded Marconi and demonstrated wireless communication possibilities.
